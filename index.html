So, Why did I chose CS?


For many that know me, it's no secret that my first passion is for animals. Horses and dogs, particularly. Because of this, many don't understand why I'm pursuing computer science and not becoming a vet or something.

To keep a long story short, I did pursue an animal based career for one and a half years. I spent my first few semesters of college at a small private university called Midway College (now Midway University) pursuing Equine Health and Rehabilitation. It was a lot of fun, and I got to learn about horses all day every day. A dream come true, right? Well, yes, but ultimately I saw this career path interfering too much with my true life goals - having time for family and my own pets. As many will tell you, a career with animals rarely leaves you with time or energy for your own furkids.

Ultimately, that year and a half after high school allowed me to learn, mature, and grow to evaluate the things that I really want in life. And these newfound priorities lead to me searching for a new career path. I researched and took a few courses that interested me to see what route I may take, and in the end, somehow I found computer science. I took Intro to Programming and instantly fell in love - finding my second passion.

Why do I love CS so much? It's challenging! I'm a person who loves learning new things and solving problems. I'm a busy bee, always looking for new tasks, new challenges, anything fun to strive for. Programming allows me to work hands-on, see instant results, and since it's always changing, I always have that new challenge. There's so many languages, so many disciplines, just so much you can do with computer science. It's crazy! It's near impossible to work with technology and be bored. There's always a new area worth exploring, and that's something many other career paths don't get to claim as highly as CS does.

All in all, programming is just plain awesome!
